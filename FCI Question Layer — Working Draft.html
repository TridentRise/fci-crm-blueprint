How to Read This Document

The Approach

This document maps question-based messaging onto the existing FCI website copy. The current headlines and text remain untouched. For each section, we show the current copy and propose a companion question that would sit alongside it — creating a "statement + question" pattern where the headline carries authority and the question creates emotional pull.

At the end, a dedicated Questions Section collects the most powerful pain-point questions into a single wall — designed to sit just before the closing CTA on the homepage.

The methodology behind this: Marcus Sheridan's "They Ask, You Answer" — a framework born from his near-bankrupt swimming pool company, River Pools & Spas. By answering every question his customers were already asking, he became the most trafficked pool website in the world. His "Big 5" topics (pricing, problems, comparisons, reviews, best-of) drive nearly all B2B purchase research. Research shows that pages opening with pain-point questions increase engagement by 36% and conversion by up to 45% compared to benefit-only copy.

New Section
Dedicated Questions Section
A wall of pain-point questions designed to sit on the homepage just before the closing CTA. No answers — or very short ones. Let the discomfort build, then offer the path forward. This is where "They Ask, You Answer" meets FCI's authority.

Design Direction

Clean, minimal layout. Questions only — no explanations, no product mentions. White or light background, generous spacing. Each question is its own line. Grouped loosely by theme but the grouping labels are subtle or absent on the live page. The section ends with a single CTA transition line.

The purpose is emotional: the reader should pause on at least two or three questions and think "I don't actually have a good answer to that." That moment of recognition is the conversion trigger.

Implementation Notes

The Statement + Question Pattern

Throughout this document, the proposed questions are designed to sit alongside the current headlines — not to replace them. The visual pattern would be: a bold declarative headline (authority, credibility) paired with an italic question in slightly smaller type (emotional pull, self-diagnosis). The headline tells them what FCI does. The question makes them feel why they need it.

In design terms, this could be a two-column layout (headline left, question right), or a stacked pattern (headline above, question below in a distinct typographic treatment). The key is that both are visible at the same time — the reader sees the authority and the challenge in one glance.

The Dedicated Questions Section

The questions wall is designed for the homepage, positioned between the "Why Trust FCI" proof section and the closing CTA. It creates a deliberate emotional shift: the reader has just seen FCI's credentials (30 years, 400+ clients, SOC 2) and now faces a cascade of questions about their own firm's readiness. The contrast between FCI's proof and their own uncertainty is the conversion mechanism.

These questions could also be repurposed as individual social media posts, email subject lines, or the opening lines of market-specific landing pages.
