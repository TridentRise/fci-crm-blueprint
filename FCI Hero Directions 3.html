Proposal 3A
Proposal 3A · Group 03 Cybersecurity Concept
The Globe — Threat intelligence map, refined

An orthographic globe slowly rotates on the right side of the hero, rendered as latitude and longitude grid lines at low opacity. Connection arcs trace great-circle paths between major financial centres — New York, London, Frankfurt, Singapore, Tokyo, Dubai — and animated data packets travel along each route in real time. The visual language is borrowed from threat intelligence platforms (Akamai, Cloudflare Radar) but stripped of urgency and drama. No red attack lines, no alarming counters. Just quiet evidence of global reach and continuous monitoring. In dark mode it reads as deep space; in light mode as a scientific illustration or cartographic reference.

Mood: global reach · quiet intelligence · always-on coverage

Proposal 3B · Group 03 Cybersecurity Concept
The Dashboard — The CISO Portal, visible behind the headline

A ghost rendering of a security operations dashboard — area charts, status indicators, metric tiles, live log entries — sits at 8–10% opacity across the right portion of the hero. The product is literally behind the message. It answers the implicit question "but what does it look like?" without making a product demo the focal point. The chart data animates slowly: a new data point appearing every few seconds, status dots blinking at irregular intervals. In dark mode the dashboard chrome glows faintly blue-white; in light mode it reads as a precise navy blueprint. The most product-specific direction in this group.

Mood: product confidence · always-running · evidence of capability

Proposal 3C
Proposal 3C · Group 03 Cybersecurity Concept
The Radar — Detection and coverage made visual

A large radar circle sits on the right side of the hero: four concentric range rings, a thin crosshair, and a slowly rotating sweep arm with a gradient fade trail. As the sweep passes, blips appear — small glowing points that linger and fade, suggesting detections. The metaphor is deliberately universal: everyone understands a radar screen as "scanning" and "finding things." For a 24×7 SOC and continuous monitoring story, it's arguably the most instinctively legible visual in this group. It has no literal cybersecurity reference — no skulls, no locks, no binary — but it communicates "always watching" more cleanly than anything else here.

Mood: always scanning · instant recognition · calm vigilance

Proposal 3D
Proposal 3D · Group 03 Cybersecurity Concept
The Hex Grid — 40,000 endpoints, made architectural

A dense honeycomb of hexagonal cells covers the hero from edge to edge — each cell representing an endpoint, a device, a user, a segment of the network. Most cells are barely-there outlines; a portion are "active" — glowing at varying intensities, pulsing gently, connected to their neighbours by faint lines. The pattern directly references FCI's core claim of 40,000+ endpoints under management. It also resonates with Zero Trust network segmentation: every cell is its own zone, every connection is deliberate. In dark mode it reads as a luminous circuit board; in light mode as a crisp architectural schematic. The most conceptually specific and intellectually interesting direction in this group.

Mood: systematic coverage · zero trust architecture · every endpoint visible
