The AI Story Arc

The story has four chapters, and each one maps directly to FCI's existing messaging pillars. The progression is natural — from using AI to protect, to using AI to analyze, to protecting clients from AI, to helping clients govern AI.
Chapter 1
AI-Driven Threat Detection
2017 – present
Pillar 1 — Cyber Tools & Settings Pillar 3 — Expert Knowledge, Applied at Scale
FCI began integrating AI into its threat detection capabilities in 2017 — years before the current wave of AI awareness in financial services. The purpose was specific: detect and block threats more accurately than signature-based or rule-based methods alone. Traditional detection relies on matching known attack patterns. AI-driven detection identifies behavioral anomalies — deviations from baseline that indicate a threat even when the attack has never been seen before.
This matters in the financial services sales office environment because the threat surface is distributed, unpredictable, and operates across devices and networks FCI does not control. Pattern-matching fails when the pattern is new. AI does not.
Proof Point Language

"FCI has deployed AI-driven threat detection since 2017 — identifying and blocking threats based on behavioral anomalies, not just known signatures. Across 40,000+ endpoints, this means threats are caught faster, with fewer false positives, and without relying on the threat having been seen before."

Chapter 2
AI-Enhanced SIEM & Log Analysis
Ongoing
Pillar 3 — Expert Knowledge, Applied at Scale Pillar 4 — Proof of Security & Compliance
FCI added AI to its Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) operations to analyze log data at a scale and speed that manual analysis cannot match. The result: patterns that indicate potential threats — lateral movement, unusual access patterns, credential misuse — are surfaced before they become incidents, and the evidence trail is preserved automatically.
This is where AI directly strengthens FCI's documentation capability. AI-enhanced SIEM does not just detect — it documents. Every flagged event, every correlated pattern, every escalation creates a forensic trail that becomes part of the compliance evidence FCI produces for the firm.
Proof Point Language

"FCI's SIEM uses AI to analyze millions of log events across the client base — finding patterns that indicate threats before they become incidents. The analysis produces a forensic trail that feeds directly into the compliance documentation regulators and cyber insurers require."

Chapter 3
AI Governance & Cloud Application Hardening
Current
Pillar 1 — Cyber Tools & Settings Pillar 2 — Procedures & Processes
The same AI tools that create business value also create risk. Financial services firms use cloud applications — Microsoft 365, CRM platforms, AI-powered productivity tools — that may expose client data to AI models if default settings are left in place. FCI's cloud application security hardening now includes reviewing and enforcing controls on how AI features interact with the firm's data.
This means configuring which AI features are enabled, which are restricted, and which are blocked entirely — based on what the firm's cybersecurity program requires and what regulators expect. The firm defines the policy. FCI implements the technical controls that enforce it.
Proof Point Language

"Cloud applications increasingly embed AI features that process data in ways firms may not expect. FCI hardens those applications — configuring, restricting, or blocking AI features to ensure the firm's client data is not exposed to models, training datasets, or third-party processing that the firm has not explicitly authorized."

Chapter 4
AI Strategy, Policy & Procedure Support
Current
Pillar 2 — Procedures & Processes Pillar 3 — Expert Knowledge, Applied at Scale
AI governance is not just a technical problem — it is a policy and procedure problem. Firms need to define how employees and affiliates may use AI tools, what data may be entered into them, what disclosures are required, and how AI-generated outputs are reviewed before use. Regulators are already asking these questions. FINRA's examination priorities reference AI governance. The SEC has signaled that AI usage in advisory communications falls under existing supervision requirements.
FCI provides support to help firms define their AI strategies, policies, and procedures. This is not FCI writing the firm's AI policy — the firm owns its program. FCI brings the expertise from working across 400+ financial services firms to help the firm make informed decisions about where AI creates value, where it creates risk, and how to document the controls that govern its use.
The enforcement can go as far as the firm's program requires. If the policy says client data must not be transferred to an AI system, FCI implements the controls that block that transfer. If the policy says specific AI tools are not authorized for use on firm or controlled endpoints, FCI blocks access to those systems entirely. The technical enforcement matches the policy — from selective restriction to full prohibition — and the documentation proves the controls are in place.
Proof Point Language

"FCI supports firms in building their AI governance framework — defining which tools are approved, what data protections apply, how AI-generated outputs are supervised, and how the policies are documented for regulators. When the firm's policy requires it, FCI enforces those decisions technically — up to and including blocking the transfer of private data to AI systems and blocking access to unauthorized AI tools on controlled endpoints. The expertise comes from what FCI sees across 400+ financial services firms navigating the same questions."
