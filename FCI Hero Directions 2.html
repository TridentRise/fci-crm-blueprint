Proposal 2A
Proposal 2A · Group 02 Photography
The Mist — Full bleed, heavy blur, felt not seen

A full-bleed photo of people in a casual business setting sits behind a heavy blur filter and a strong color overlay — so strong that you only feel the human presence rather than see it. In dark mode the overlay is deep navy at 78% opacity; in light mode it shifts to pale blue-white at 80%. What comes through is warmth: the suggestion of movement, the glow of ambient light, the unmistakable feeling that there are real people behind this. No face is readable. Nothing is literal. The brand color and typography take complete precedence. This is the most conservative photo treatment — the safest choice if the concern is being too obviously "stock photo."

Mood: warmth underneath precision · human-backed · subtle presence

Proposal 2B
Proposal 2B · Group 02 Photography
The Window — Half-reveal, sharp, desaturated

The left half of the hero is pure brand color — a clean canvas for the wordmark and copy. The right half gradually reveals a desaturated photo of people in a team setting, fading in smoothly from the left edge. The photo is sharp but stripped of most color so it harmonizes with the palette rather than competing with it. In dark mode it reads as deep and architectural; in light mode the people are more visible on the pale background — closer to a magazine editorial layout. The composition creates a natural left-right reading path: message first, human context second. This is the most structured and readable direction.

Mood: editorial clarity · structured humanity · message-first

Proposal 2C
Proposal 2C · Group 02 Photography
The Watermark — Ghost presence, almost invisible

No blur this time — the photo sits at full resolution at 17–22% opacity, so heavily desaturated it reads as pure texture. Faces are discernible only if you look for them; the dominant experience is color field, not photography. This creates a layered effect: at a glance it's a clean brand page; on closer inspection there's a human scene embedded in the background like a watermark. The light-mode version is more ghost-like; dark mode brings slightly more of the scene through. A very restrained and Zen treatment — the closest to having no photo at all, while still being warmer than a pure abstract hero.

Mood: ghost warmth · layered depth · Zen restraint

Proposal 2D
Proposal 2D · Group 02 Photography
The Duotone — Monochromatic blue, fully brand-matched

The photo is converted to grayscale via CSS filter, then a deep navy or electric blue overlay shifts everything into a monochromatic blue palette — the same blue as FCI's brand. The result is unmistakably a photo (you can see people, movement, depth) but it reads entirely as color rather than image. In dark mode the overlay is near-navy, very architectural and serious. In light mode the overlay is electric blue at a lighter opacity, and the people come through with more warmth and visibility — almost like a blue-tinted glass wall. This is the most visually distinctive approach: boldest, most brand-committed, and — once it becomes the site identity — the most recognizable.

Mood: bold identity · human through the brand · visually unforgettable
