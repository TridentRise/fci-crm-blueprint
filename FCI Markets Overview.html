Who We Serve

Website copy for /markets — the landing page that introduces all three markets FCI serves and routes visitors to the right conversation.

FCI implements cybersecurity controls and produces evidence of compliance for firms in regulated financial services. Every market we serve shares the same structural problem: the home office sets the security program, but the sales offices, branches, and field agents operate outside direct IT control. FCI closes that gap.
Market 1
Broker-Dealers & Branch Offices
The broker-dealer home office sets the compliance program. That program must reach every branch and every registered representative — including those working from independent offices or home locations. FCI enforces controls across the full branch network and produces FINRA and SEC-aligned evidence continuously, so the home office can confirm every branch is in good order before the examiner asks.

What Every Market Shares

Regardless of the specific regulatory framework, every firm FCI serves faces the same structural challenge: the home office or headquarters defines the cybersecurity program, but the field — branches, agencies, advisor offices — operates outside direct IT control. The gap between the program on paper and what can be verified in the field is where regulatory exposure lives.

FCI closes that gap by implementing controls at the endpoint level across the entire distributed operation, then producing evidence of those controls continuously through the CISO Portal. The firm's compliance team does not prepare for exams. They open a living record that has been building itself since the day the service went live.

Every FCI engagement covers the same core: managed endpoint protection, Zero Trust enforcement, compliance documentation, and 24/7 SOC monitoring. The difference between markets is which regulators are asking and which frameworks apply — not what FCI deploys.
