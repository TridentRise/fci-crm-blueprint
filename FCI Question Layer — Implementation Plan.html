Question Layer — Implementation Plan
A phased plan for integrating question-based messaging across the FCI brand system. Based on the approved Working Draft and Marcus Sheridan's "They Ask, You Answer" methodology.
Overview
What We're Doing and Why
Adding a consistent question layer across FCI's brand materials — not replacing existing copy, but adding a new dimension that creates emotional pull alongside the authority of what's already written.

The Principle

A statement tells the prospect what FCI does. A question makes the prospect feel why they need it. The two work together: the headline carries credibility, the companion question forces the reader to mentally answer — and most of them realize the answer is "no, we can't do that today." That moment of recognition is the conversion trigger.

Every question element will have a consistent, recognizable visual treatment across all materials — website, white papers, brochures — so that visitors and readers learn to associate this design pattern with FCI's approach of asking the hard questions the industry avoids.

Scope

Six deliverables across two phases. Phase 1 establishes the design system and applies the question layer to the two strategic documents (Messaging Architecture and Website Copy). Phase 2 extends the pattern to the two finalized brochures (CISO Portal White Paper and Security Assessment). All other collateral — one-pagers, presentations — will be rebuilt from scratch and will adopt the question layer natively when that happens.

1
Messaging Architecture — Question Layer Section
FCI Messaging Architecture.html

Add a new section to the Messaging Architecture titled "The Question Layer" that defines this approach as a strategic brand element — not just a copywriting technique, but a deliberate positioning choice. The section establishes why FCI uses questions (the Sheridan methodology, the psychology of prospect self-diagnosis), where questions appear (inline companion pattern, dedicated question blocks), and how they look visually.

This section also contains the approved question bank — the master list of questions organized by theme and by market. Every other document pulls its questions from this bank, ensuring consistency. When a question is refined or replaced, it's updated here first.

Tasks

Finalize the question bank from the Working Draft — organized by theme (regulatory readiness, breach preparedness, compliance operations, policy vs. proof, cyber insurance) and tagged by market applicability (all markets, broker-dealers & branch offices, insurance carriers & agencies, RIAs)
Add the section to FCI Messaging Architecture.html and verify it reads as a natural extension of the existing framework
2
Website Copy — Inline Questions + Dedicated Section
FCI Website Copy.html

Integrate the question layer into the website copy document. This is the largest integration — it touches all four pages (homepage, broker-dealers & branch offices, insurance carriers & agencies, RIAs). Two types of additions:

Inline companion questions: For each major section, add the approved question alongside the existing headline. The existing headline text does not change. The question is a new element that appears in a consistent visual treatment (defined in Deliverable 1). The Website Copy document will include design notes for each placement showing exactly where the question sits relative to the headline.

Dedicated Questions Section: A new section on the homepage, positioned between "Why Trust FCI" and the closing CTA. Contains the wall of pain-point questions grouped by theme, ending with the transition line ("If you paused on any of these — that is exactly why FCI exists") leading into the CTA.

Tasks
Add companion questions to all homepage sections (hero, problem, four pillars, who we serve, security assessment, managed cybersecurity, CISO Portal spotlight, why trust FCI, closing CTA)
Add companion questions to the Broker-Dealers & Branch Offices page (hero, problem, what changes, exam evidence)
Add companion questions to the Insurance Carriers & Agencies page (hero, problem, what changes, evidence section)
Add companion questions to the RIAs page (hero, problem, what changes, examiner section)
Add the dedicated Questions Section to the homepage with design direction notes
Review all additions to confirm questions pull from the approved bank (Deliverable 1) and no existing copy was modified

Depends on: Deliverable 1 (Messaging Architecture) — the question bank and visual spec must be finalized before questions are added to the website copy, so all placements are consistent.

3
CISO Portal White Paper — Question Layer Integration
CISO_Portal_White_Paper.html

The CISO Portal White Paper has six sections with strong headlines that are perfect candidates for companion questions. The document already has a pull quote style and challenge grid — the question pattern will complement these existing elements without competing with them.

Inline placements — companion questions alongside these existing headlines:

"Most firms are managing compliance the hard way." → question about how many hours the reader's team spends

"One platform. Everything your security officer needs to act." → question about how many systems their security officer currently juggles

"Know exactly where you stand — and what to fix first." → question about whether they can see their compliance posture right now

"Tasks that used to take hours now take seconds." → question about their current decommissioning workflow

"Tangible benefits from day one." → question about what their team would do with 80% of their compliance time back

"Evidence that meets modern examiner expectations." → question about whether their current evidence would satisfy an examiner today

Dedicated questions block — a compact callout box before the closing panel, containing 4–6 questions specific to the CISO Portal's value proposition (compliance visibility, device management, incident response, exam readiness).

Tasks
Draft companion questions for the six section headlines, pulling from the approved bank where applicable and writing CISO Portal-specific questions where needed
Draft the dedicated questions block (4–6 questions focused on portal capabilities)
Add both elements to CISO_Portal_White_Paper.html using the visual spec from Deliverable 1
Verify the additions do not disrupt the white paper's narrative flow — the question elements should enhance, not interrupt

Depends on: Deliverable 1 (visual spec and question bank finalized).

Security Assessment Brochure — Question Layer Integration
FCI Security Assessment.html

Inline placements — companion questions alongside section titles and layer headings:

Brand Guidelines — Question Element Spec
FCI Brand Guidelines.html

If the question layer becomes a signature brand element — and it should — it needs a slot in the Brand Guidelines. This doesn't need to be a large section: a brief entry under the typography or UI elements area that defines the question's visual treatment (font weight, style, color, border accent, spacing) with a "do / don't" example showing how the pattern works in context.

This ensures that when the one-pagers and other collateral get rebuilt from scratch, anyone producing those materials knows exactly how the question element should look — even if they weren't part of this conversation.
