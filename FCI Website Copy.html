What Is the NIST Framework?
Intro Copy

The NIST Cybersecurity Framework (NIST CSF) is the standard that regulators, examiners, and cyber insurers use to evaluate whether a firm's security program is real. SEC examiners reference it. Page 7 — Solutions
Zero Trust
Explains Zero Trust in plain language for financial firm executives. Focuses on containing field exposure — not technical architecture.
Page Hero
H1

Verify every user, device, and network. Before they reach anything.

Sub-Head

Zero Trust is not a product. It is a principle: nothing on your network is trusted by default. Every access request is verified — user identity, device health, location, and context — before a connection is allowed. FCI deploys this model pragmatically for firms with distributed field operations.

Why This Matters for Your Firm
Section Headline

When one device is compromised, Zero Trust determines how far the damage spreads.

Body Copy

In a traditional network model, a compromised device in a branch office or field agency has access to everything that device user normally reaches. Client data. Internal systems. Other network connections.

In a Zero Trust model, that same device — once flagged — can reach nothing it has not been explicitly authorized to access. The blast radius of any incident is contained at the source.

For financial firms with dozens or hundreds of field locations, Zero Trust is not a luxury. It is the architecture that makes distributed operations defensible.

How FCI Deploys Zero Trust
Always-On VPN

Every advisor and rep session runs through FCI's managed VPN — automatically, without requiring the user to connect manually. Every session is encrypted. Every connection is logged. Regulators see consistent, verifiable evidence of encrypted access across your entire network.

Identity Verification

Multi-factor authentication enforced on every device and every access point. Not optional. Not user-configured. Deployed and maintained by FCI — consistently, without drift.

Device Trust

Every device requesting access to firm systems is verified against a current health baseline — encryption status, patch level, endpoint protection active. Devices that fall out of compliance are flagged before they create exposure.

Access Segmentation

Access is granted by role, office, and supervisor — mapped from your directory services. Field advisors reach what they need. They cannot reach what they don't. A compromise in one location stays in one location.

Our Story
Body Copy

Financial services sales offices present a security challenge that most managed security service providers have never fully understood. Independent advisors, appointed agents, branch offices with their own devices and their own networks — the sales office model creates an attack surface that was never designed for centralized IT management.

A typical MSSP deploys endpoint protection or runs a SOC — then hands the client a dashboard and a phone number. The client is still responsible for configuring controls, writing policies, producing compliance evidence, and responding to regulatory examinations. FCI does all of that. The four pillars — Cyber Tools & Settings, Procedures & Processes, Expert Knowledge Applied at Scale, and Proof of Security & Compliance — are not sold separately. They are one service, delivered by one team.

Over 30 years, we have developed the operational expertise, regulatory fluency, and purpose-built technology that this market requires. Our CISO Portal was designed specifically for firms managing compliance across sales office networks. Our SOC analysts understand the regulatory context of what they are protecting. Our implementation approach is calibrated to financial firms that cannot afford user friction or IT downtime.

Today we manage more than 40,000 endpoints across more than 400 clients — registered investment advisers, broker-dealers, and insurance carriers and agencies. We hold SOC 2 Type 1 certification and a 100% SecurityScorecard rating. We are independently verified, not self-described.

What Makes FCI Different
Body Copy — For the skeptical buyer

Most managed security service providers serve financial services as one vertical among many. They deploy endpoint protection or run a SOC — then hand the client a dashboard and a phone number. They do not produce the documentation regulators actually ask for. They do not sit with the firm during an examination. They do not manage incident response. They do not enforce controls across devices they did not sell.

FCI does all four — because the financial services sales office requires all four. Our managed service was designed from the beginning for the sales office model — the BYOD environments, the independent branch offices, the compliance requirements that extend to every appointed agent and registered rep, not just the home office.

This specificity is not a marketing claim. It is operational DNA. Our tools, our workflows, our SOC analysts, and our CISO Portal are all built around one question: what does it take to keep a financial services sales office compliant, protected, and exam-ready — continuously?
